Motors On from the GCC
Props Off, on the Bench

Connect the Ground Control Center to the AeroSync 5 over its DroneBridge link, watch live flight-controller telemetry, and spin a motor from a GCC button. No flying this phase: props off, drone secured.

Drone CC3D Revo Mini + ESP32 DroneBridge Link MAVLink UDP over Wi-Fi Goal command pipeline proven, props off
Safety, before anything else
  • Remove all four propellers. Every step here assumes bare motors.
  • Secure the drone so it cannot move: clamp it or strap the frame to something heavy. Armed motors produce thrust and torque even without props.
  • Keep hands, hair, and cables clear of the motors whenever the battery is connected.
  • Know the kill switch: the red DISARM button (top-right of the Drone tab) always works while connected and stops the motors immediately.
  • Connect the LiPo only when you reach the step that needs it, and disconnect it as soon as you are done.

01

Update and run the GCC

The Drone tab is new code, so pull the latest and rebuild on the Windows laptop:

PowerShell · drone-network-system\gcc_app
PS> git pull
PS> flutter pub get
PS> flutter run -d windows

The Drone tab in the nav rail is now a real cockpit, not the old locked placeholder.

02

Confirm the MAVLink link and note the address

You already confirmed the flight controller speaks MAVLink. Just note the DroneBridge Wi-Fi details you will use:

  • The ESP32 broadcasts a Wi-Fi network (commonly named "DroneBridge for ESP32"). Note its SSID.
  • Its MAVLink endpoint is usually 192.168.2.1:14550. If your DroneBridge web UI (browse to the ESP32's IP) shows a different UDP client port or IP, note that instead.
If you want to double-check end to end first, connect Mission Planner to that same Wi-Fi / UDP and confirm telemetry, then close it (only one GCS can hold the link at a time).
03

Secure the drone, props off

Physically prepare the drone:

  1. Confirm all four props are OFF.
  2. Clamp or strap the frame down so it cannot flip or slide.
  3. Have the charged LiPo ready but not yet connected.
  4. Clear the area around the motors.
Do not skip this
  • A 2200 kV motor at even low throttle is loud and forceful. Props off plus secured frame is the whole safety margin for a bench test.
04

Join DroneBridge and connect the GCC

  1. On the laptop, join the ESP32 DroneBridge Wi-Fi network. (It has no internet; that is fine.)
  2. In the GCC Settings tab, find MAVLink target. Tap the DroneBridge direct chip (fills udp:192.168.2.1:14550), or type your address and press Enter.
  3. Go to the Drone tab and tap Connect.
Drone tab, top bar

Shows the target and a Connect / Disconnect button. After Connect, a link-status card appears below it.

Now connect the LiPo to the drone so the flight controller powers up and starts sending MAVLink (the ESP32 may run from USB, but the FC needs the battery/BEC).

05

Gate — live telemetry

Expected on the Drone tab
MAVLink heartbeat live. Commands enabled.   (green card)
Telemetry:  DISARMED
  Mode STABILIZE   Battery 11.4 V   GPS no fix (bench)   Attitude R/P/Y

The green heartbeat card is the gate: it means a heartbeat arrived within the last 2 seconds. Battery voltage should match your pack. No GPS fix indoors is normal. If you tilt the drone, the Attitude numbers should change — a good sign the FC is really talking.

Stop here

Confirm the heartbeat card is green and telemetry is updating (battery voltage present, attitude changes when you tilt the frame). If it stays red ("waiting for heartbeat"), the link is not up — see troubleshooting; do not proceed to motor test until telemetry is live.

06

Gate — spin a motor from the GCC

This is the milestone: the GCC turns a motor on. The motor test spins ONE motor briefly at low throttle and needs no arming.

  1. Final check: props off, frame secured, everyone clear.
  2. In the Commands card, tap Motor 1. Confirm the dialog.
  3. Motor 1 should spin for ~3 seconds at low throttle, then stop on its own.
  4. Try Motor 2/3/4 to confirm each esc/motor responds.
Expected
Last command (209): accepted        (green tick under the buttons)
[6] motor test               (a status line from the FC, maybe)

"209" is DO_MOTOR_TEST; "accepted" is the FC acknowledging. The motor spinning is the physical proof. If a motor does not spin but the command was accepted, that motor/ESC may be the issue, not the link.

Stop here — this is the deliverable

A motor spun on a command sent from your Ground Control Center, over Wi-Fi MAVLink, props off. That is "operate the drone motors from the GCC" achieved. If anything feels wrong, hit the red DISARM button or pull the battery. Record which motors responded for the report.

07

Arm / disarm from the GCC (optional)

Arming makes all motors live at once, so the motor test above is the safer proof. If you want to show arming too:

  1. Tap Arm and confirm. Watch the status log: ArduPilot will either arm (the DISARMED chip flips to ARMED) or refuse and print WHY (e.g. "PreArm: ..."). Those messages are exactly what the log is for.
  2. With no RC transmitter, arming may be refused until bench-only parameters are relaxed in Mission Planner (ARMING_CHECK and RC/throttle-failsafe params). Those settings are not airworthy and must be reverted before any flight — see docs/DRONE_LINK.md section 4.
  3. Tap Disarm (or the red kill button) to stand down.
Guided "go to marker" flight is intentionally out of scope this phase: it needs an outdoor GPS fix, tuning, and failsafes a bench racing quad does not have yet. The Drone tab shows it as an unavailable stretch, which is the honest state.
08

Scope note and troubleshooting

Why only the direct link? The system drone's Pi has one radio and no mesh adapter, so it cannot join the 2.4 GHz DTN backbone while also reaching the 2.4 GHz ESP32. Control is therefore direct over DroneBridge; the mesh-relayed path is documented as future work in docs/DRONE_LINK.md. This does not affect victims joining the drone APs or DRONE_A/B syncing.

SymptomTry this
Heartbeat card stays redConfirm the laptop is on the DroneBridge Wi-Fi, the target matches the ESP32 (default 192.168.2.1:14550), and the FC is powered (battery/BEC, not just USB). Only one GCS can hold the link — close Mission Planner.
"Could not open the link"Another app is bound to the MAVLink UDP port, or the address is wrong. Close other GCS software and re-tap Connect.
Telemetry frozen / commands greyed outThe heartbeat went stale (>2 s), which disables commands by design. Check Wi-Fi range to the drone; the buttons re-enable when the heartbeat returns.
Motor test accepted but no spinLink is fine; suspect that motor/ESC or a low battery. Try another motor number to compare.
Arm refused with a PreArm messageRead the message in the status log — it names the exact check. With no RC, relax the bench-only params (DRONE_LINK.md sec 4); revert before flight.
